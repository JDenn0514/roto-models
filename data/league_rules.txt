  Q: VERY IMPORTANT question about "waivers": Sometimes there is some confusion about the meaning of "waivers" for leagues new to our site, especially if they have used one of the larger, Media-based sites in the past (like Yahoo). At OnRoto, players not on the roster of any team in a league are generally considered to be what we call "free agents." Leagues may, however, treat certain types of untaken players differently -- as being "on waivers" instead of being "free agents." In most cases, "waivers" refers to only those players who were recently dropped by a team in the league, though sometimes "waivers" refers to players recently called up or traded in from the other MLB league. Note that in leagues which use "waivers," the rules governing how teams pick up players on waivers are different from the rules governing how free agents are picked up -- there's no point in making a distinction otherwise. Everyone at OnRoto uses "free agents", but only leagues which wish to distinguish between free agents and recently cut or added players use "waivers". Do you use waivers? If this question is at all confusing or refers to any concept you are not familiar with, you almost certainly do not use waivers.
  A: We use both a free agent pool and a waiver wire

  Q: A very small number of leagues (4, so far), distinguish between players who are waived and players who are "dropped" or released. Do you use this option? On a later set-up page, you can specify that waived minor leaguers and DL players are not eligible to be claimed, and so are, in effect, dropped.
  A: No, we do not use this.  All players who are cut are waived

  Q: The Ohtani question: How does your league plan to treat potential two-way player Shohei Ohtani this year?
  A: As two completely distinct players, Hitter Ohtani and Pitcher Ohtani, who can be on different teams

  Q: How will you permit his owner to use Ohtani this year? Note that if you select the first option, when Ohtani is active as a hitter AND a pitcher at the same time, he will occupy two of his owner's active roster slots - one hitter slot AND one pitcher slot.
  A: As either a hitter or a pitcher -- or both at the same time

  Q: How many total slots does Ohtani count against your roster limits? There are three options. From the feedback we received, we think that most leagues will want Ohtani to be count as just one player (except when both his hitter and pitcher halves are active at the same time, if that is permitted). In this option, when Ohtani is active as either a hitter or pitcher, his other half will not count as a bench player against your reserve limit; similarly, when both his selves are reserved, he'll count as just one bench player. The second option is for leagues which have suggested they want Ohtani to be counted as taking up two roster slots regardless of whether he's active or not. In this option, when Ohtani is active as a hitter or a pitcher, his other half still counts as a reserve player. Finally, we've had enough requests to permit Ohtani to count as just one player even when he's active as both a hitter and a pitcher that we have decided to include that option. But in this case, both Ohtanis MUST be active or must be reserved. It is not permitted to have his pitcher half active and his hitter half reserved for example. And in this case, his hitter half will count against the roster, and his pitcher half will not. So in effect, you will get a free extra pitcher.
  A: One slot (although 2 if it's allowed to have both Ohtanis active at the same time)

  Q: How do you wish to handle trades on the site?
  A: Owners propose/accept trades on the site. Trades accepted by all parties must then be approved by the commissioner

  A: Don't disable trading of draft picks

Example: Suppose Monday is your transaction day, and on Saturday a trade is made (effective Monday, of course) in which one team receives some FAAB dollars. Can they use that money for Monday's bids, or must they wait until the following Monday before the money is available?
  A: No, they don't have to wait -- the money is available right away

  A: The All-Star Break

  Q: What is the September date after which Owner-to-Owner trades are no longer allowed?
  A: 07.01

  Q: After a trade is accepted by all teams involved, when is the deadline for the other owners to vote on it?

  A: Failure to vote is considered a vote in favor of a trade

  Q: What fraction of teams must approve a trade in order for it to pass?
  A: All of the teams in the league

  Q: Would you like to see a list of which owners voted to approve and deny the trades being voted on?
  A: No, keep the voting anonymous

  Q: One (1) league has a special rule whereby the weekly trade deadline is tied to the Bid-Meister deadline. Any trade made between the Bid-Meister deadline and the line-up change deadline is pushed to the following week. Is this league yours?

  A: 1

  A: No -- only whole dollar values

  WAIVER RULES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Q: How are waiver claims awarded?
  A: By priority, last place team gets one choice before others choose

  Q: Is there a limit on the number of waiver claims a team may be awarded during any one transaction cycle?
  A: No

  Q: What is the yearly limit on the number of waiver claims per team?
  A: 1

  Q: In some leagues, if a team submits a claim on a player that they thelmselves waived during the previous transaction period, their claim has the lowest priority on that player. Do you use this rule?
  A: No, priority is independent of which team waived the player

  Q: How long is a player on waivers? This question is mainly relevant to leagues with daily transactions and leagues with different transaction cycles for adding free agents and waiver claims. Almost all leagues with weekly waiver claims will want to select "One full transaction cycle." If you allow transactions everyday but have weekly waiver claims and need all waivers to expire on the same day of the week, select the last option, "It varies, depending on when the player was waived."
  A: One full transaction cycle (one day for leagues with daily transactions)

  Q: One (1) league in our combined 35+ years of experience in rotisserie baseball allows waived players to be claimed the same transaction period that they were waived (essentially using zero-day waivers). Is this league you (hint: NO)?
  A: No

  Q: Can a player be claimed before his waivers expire -- such as during the first week of a two-cycle waiver period, or on day 1 of a 3-day waiver period?
  A: No

  Q: When players are waived in between the waiver claim cycles, do their waivers expire at the first waiver period 7 days after he was waived, or at the very next waiver claim date? For example, if your next waiver claims are set for 5/10 and a player is waived on 5/8, do his waivers run through 5/17, or do his waivers expire on 5/10?
  A: waivers run through the first claim cycle 7 days after he was waived

  Q: If a player on waivers is sent to the minors or placed on the DL:
  A: He is no longer eligible to be claimed off waivers

  Q: If a player traded to the "other" league is waived or has been waived by his current owner, is he eligible to be claimed?
  A: No -- once he's waived, he not eligible in our league

  Q: In a small number of leagues, players just called up from the minors or recently traded in from the opposite league go immediately to the waiver wire instead of the free agent pool. Do you use this rule?
  A: No

  Q: The default in weekly transaction leagues forces players new to the league to be on waivers for at least one full transaction cycle before they can be claimed. This means a player added to the wire the day after the regular claim day could sit on waivers as long as 13 days. A few leagues have requested that players be made available for claim during the first cycle they're on waivers -- at little as half a day, if they're call up the morning your claims run. Do you want to use this 'immediately claimable' option?
  A: No, we want players new to the league to be on waivers for at least one week before they can be added

  Q: When a player is added to a team as a waiver claim:
  A: He must be added to the active roster / starting line-up and must remain there for one transaction cycle

  Q: How do you determine the salary of a player claimed on waivers?
  A: His salary on his previous team

  Q: What is the default contract year of a player added as a waiver claim?
  A: Same as the free agent default

  Q: In legacy leagues, do players waived in the pre-season (before draft-day rosters are set), stay on waivers so they may be claimed by other teams prior to the draft?
  A: No

  Q: In legacy leagues, do players waived in the post-season (after the final day), stay on waivers so they may be claimed by other teams?
  A: No

For example, you couldn't say "bid $5 on player A, and if I don't get him, bid $10 on player B." But we know there are times when owners legitimately want to increase their bids, and we've also improved the sort program so it should now converge most of the time when it sees this. Therefore, we're offering an option to allow higher value bids in the same block. Do you want to permit owners to increase their bids on lower priority players within the same bid block (saying "yes" allows this)?
  A: No

  A: No -- we want the default, which is to allow this

  Q: Do you want to continue to run the Bid-Meister in the post-season, after the season is over?
  A: No

  Q: If you use the Bid-Meister in the post-season and you use FAAB, do you need to reset the FAAB spent to $0?
  A: No

  Q: A handful of leagues have requested that the full set of bids not be exposed after the Bid Meister has run, so that any contingent bids that ended up not being necessary remain hidden from the rest of the league.
